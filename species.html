Here you can learn about some of the many types of succulents the world has to offer. It would be nearly impossible for us to list them all, however we have provided some of our favorites and the most popular bunch. If you are a beginner and just wanting to know a bit more about plant identification this may help a bit.

  1. Sedum
    Sedum are some of the most popular types of succulent as they tend to be easy to care for and provided in many places. They are a large part of the family Crassulaceae and often bear flowers. Sedum are a great beginer plant as most of the ones found in shops and stores are easy to maintain and grow fairly quick. Many people know them as "stone crops" as they are often used to cover ground and are perrenials.

    Sedum Rubrotinctum (Jelly Bean Sedum)

    Sedum sieboldii variegatum

    Sedum Morghanium (Donkey Tail)

    Sedum glaucophyllum

    Sedum Confusum

    Sedum Sichotense


  2. Echeveria
    Echeveria are characterized by their rosette-like appearance. They are often included in succulent bouqets which are increasingly gaining popularity. They are also a member of the Crassulaceae family and also bear flowers. They have some of the fleshiest leaves however many of them are a bit harder to take care of if you want to maintain their perfect rosette appearance.

    Echeveria Agavoides (Lipstick Echeveria)

    Echeveria Imbricata

    Echevaria Elegans

    Echeveria Purpusorum

    Echeveria Black Prince

    Ruffled Echeveria

    Echevaria Raindrop


  3. Kalanchoe
    Kalanchoe are also of the family Crassulaceae and are characterized by their usually large leaves. They are also perrenials and are easy to care for as you can easily tell when they need to be watered by the thickness of their leaves. The kalanchoe includes the interesting "panda" plant which is soft to the touch.

    Kalanchoe thyrsiflora (Flap Jack Kalanchoe)

    Kalanchoe Blossfienda

    Kalanchoe Beharensis

    Kalanchoe Tomentosa (Panda Plant)

    Kalanchoe Cotelydon (Bear Paw)


  4. Haworthia
    Haworthia are from the family Xanthorrhoeaceae and are known to often resemble miniature aloes. Their leaves are often sturdy and many have ridges or bumps, while others have translucent coloring. They are mainly found in dark green colors.

    Haworthia Retusa

    Haworthia Zebrina

    Haworthia Tessellata

    Haworthia Truncata


  5. Lithops
    Lithops are from the family Aizoaceae and are often known as "living stones". These plants are among the hardest to maintain as they are incredibly prome to over watering however they are some of our favorites. They produce bright white flowers and split to reproduce.

    Fenestria (Baby Toes)

    Lithops Hookeri

    Pleiospilos Nelii (SplitRock)

    Royal Flush



  6. Crassula
    Crassula from the Crassulaceae family often have smaller leaves and form interesting geometric patterns. Although found in many places of the world, they are native to east and South Africa.

    Crassula Ovata (Jade)

    Crassula Gollum

    Crassula Corymbulosa

    Crassula Perforate (String of Buttons)
